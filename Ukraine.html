JOURNAL ON UKRAINE

The Journal seeks to preserve the Ukrainian people's heritage, identity and cultural memory, and to protect these from revision and repression, through historical and political analysis, cultural commentary and personal reflection.

The Ukrainian people, their land and liberty, are now being devastated, their borders and history are now being erased, following Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine in February 2022. Journal on Ukraine is Bridge to Literacy's response to the Russian invasion and its contribution to Ukraine in its struggle for independence.

War is as much a strife for land as for historical, national and cultural identity. Journal on Ukraine covers the Russo-Ukrainian War's cultural front, where anyone, through strength of thought, can fight, and where words are the only means to victory.

Journal on Ukraine accepts articles, essays, editorials and other prose on any topic related to Ukraine, Ukraine's heritage, culture and politics, the history of these, and Russo-Ukrainian War's impact on them. Abstract essays on themes of national identity, culture, art, language and the effect of war in shaping, suppressing and inspiring all these are likewise welcome.
